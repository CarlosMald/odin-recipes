Lasagna Recipe

Description

This is our world-famous three cheese lasagna recipe loved by well-known TV personality Garfield!

Ingredients

  • 1 lb. ground beef
  • 1 jar RAGU® Kettle Cooked Roasted Garlic Sauce
  • 1 container (15 oz.) part-skim Ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded part-skim Mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese, divided
  • 2 eggs
  • 12 lasagna noodles, cooked and drained

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Brown ground beef in 12-inch skillet; drain. Stir in sauce; heat through.
  2. Combine ricotta cheese, mozzarella cheese, 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese and eggs in large bowl; set aside.
  3. Evenly spread 1 cup meat sauce in 13 x 9-inch baking dish. Layer 4 lasagna noodles, then 1 cup meat sauce and 1/2 of the ricotta cheese mixture; repeat. Top with remaining 4 noodles and sauce. Cover with aluminum foil and bake 30 minutes. Remove foil and sprinkle with remaining 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese. Bake uncovered an additional 5 minutes. Let stand 10 minutes before serving.

Pupusa Recipe

Description

This is the national dish of El Salvador, made with corn flour, it resembles a tortilla typically filled with cheese, pork, and beans as well as other options.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups maseca flour
  • 2 cups warm water
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup refried beans
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (such as mozzarella or monterey jack)
  • 1 cup cooked and seasoned pork, loroco, or other fillings of your choice

Directions

  1. In a large bowl, mix the maseca corn flower with a bit of warm water, gradually adding more water and salt until the flour is soft and malleable. Let it rest for 15 to 20 minutes, covered with a cloth.
  2. Once it is done resting, shape the dough into small balls these size of a golf ball. Tip: You can wet your hands with a bit of water or oil for this step.
  3. To fill the dough, use your finger to make small hole in the dough until you have a big enough hole for your fillings.
  4. Add approximately 1 tablespoon of cheese and beans (or other preferred filling) to the hole. Close the hole carefully and reform the ball of dough with your hands.
  5. Cook the pupusas on a skillet for approximately 2-4 minutes on each side.
  6. Serve with a side of salsa and curtido (a pickled cabbage dish).

Smashburger Recipe

Description

This is a fun twist on a traditional burger, with much of the same flavor characteristics but a slightly crispier texture from the smashed patty.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb. ground beef, 80-20 or 85-15.
  • Lowry's Seasoned Salt and Garlic Salt
  • 4 slices of cheddar cheese
  • 4 hamburger buns

Directions

  1. Divide the ground beef into 4 equal portions and shape each into a circular ball shape with your hands.
  2. Preheat a skillet or griddle over medium-high heat.
  3. Lightly season the beef balls with the seasoned salt and garlic salt until the top side is evenly covered in seasoning.
  4. Place each beef portion top-side down onto the skillet to ensure the seasoned side cooks first.
  5. Season the side facing up with the same proportions of seasoned salt and garlic salt, then using your spatula or a flat utensil, smash the burgers onto the skillet until they are about 1/2 inch thick.
  6. Cook the burgers for approximately 2-3 minutes on the first side, then flip and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes on the second side. Add a slice of cheddar cheese to each burger during the last minute of cooking to allow it to melt.
  7. Toast the buns in an oven for 2-3 minutes until lightly browned.
  8. Assemble the burgers with your preferred toppings and enjoy!
